# TRIO SOLAR — D2D Sales Operations Intelligence Report
## METHOD Team · Bay Area, CA · Aug 2025 – Mar 2026

---

## Executive Summary

The Trio Solar dashboard provides a comprehensive operational view of METHOD's door-to-door solar sales performance across 10 Bay Area cities. Over an 8-month period, the team has signed **66 deals** with an approximate **22% cancellation rate**, generating an estimated **~$295k in revenue** from installed systems.

**Key insight:** Lower-income PG&E markets (Hayward, Richmond, Oakland) drive the highest deal volume but carry elevated cancellation and property risk. The team has demonstrated significant operational improvement, reducing average install time by **47%** from ~96 days to ~51 days.

---

## 1. KPI Overview (Top Cards)

Six headline metrics providing an at-a-glance health check of the sales operation:

| Metric | Description | Business Value |
|--------|-------------|----------------|
| **Total Signed** | All deals signed to date (66) | Team productivity benchmark |
| **Installed & Paid** | Deals fully completed and revenue collected | Realized revenue indicator |
| **Cancellation Rate** | % of signed deals that cancelled (~22%) | Deal quality and customer qualification effectiveness |
| **Total Revenue** | Sum of installed deal values | Top-line financial performance |
| **Avg Deal Value** | Revenue per installed deal | Pricing strategy effectiveness |
| **Avg Install Time** | Days from signing to installation complete | Operational efficiency metric |

**How to use:** Compare month-over-month to detect trends. A rising cancellation rate may indicate territory saturation or qualification issues. Declining average deal value may signal a shift toward smaller residential systems.

## 2. Monthly Pipeline Overview (Stacked Bar Chart)

Displays deal volume by month, segmented into **Signed**, **Installed**, and **Cancelled** categories.

**What it reveals:**
- **Seasonal patterns:** Dec–Jan show reduced activity (winter slowdown, holiday season), while Oct and Feb–Mar show strong ramp-up periods
- **Conversion lag:** Earlier months show higher installation rates (more time to complete), while recent months have more pipeline deals
- **Mar 2026 surge:** 14 deals signed indicates accelerating momentum

**Business value:** Enables capacity planning and rep staffing decisions. If March's pace continues, the team may need additional installation crew bandwidth. The visible conversion lag helps set realistic revenue expectations for recent signings.

---

## 3. Average Install Time Trend (Line Chart)

Tracks the monthly average number of days from deal signing to completed installation.

**Key data points:**
- **Aug 2025:** ~96 days (baseline)
- **Feb 2026:** ~51 days
- **Reduction:** 47% improvement over 7 months

**Click-to-drill:** Clicking any data point reveals city-level install time breakdown for that month, showing which markets are faster or slower than average.

**Business value:**
- **Faster revenue realization:** Each day saved in install time accelerates cash flow
- **Operational maturity signal:** Consistent month-over-month reduction demonstrates process improvements (permitting efficiency, contractor coordination, design turnaround)
- **Forecasting:** The trend line enables prediction of future install timelines for pipeline deals
- **City-level drill-down** identifies specific markets causing delays, enabling targeted process improvements

## 5. Revenue Distribution (Histogram)

Displays the frequency distribution of deal values with **mean** and **median** markers overlaid.

**What it reveals:**
- The **sweet spot** for deal pricing falls in the $5,500–$9,500 range (highest frequency)
- A small tail of premium deals ($12,500+) pulls the mean above the median
- Very few deals below $4,500, indicating effective minimum deal size qualification

**Business value:**
- **Pricing strategy validation:** Confirms the team is consistently closing within the target range
- **Outlier awareness:** Premium deals may require different sales approaches or customer profiles
- **Commission modeling:** The distribution shape informs realistic per-rep revenue projections

## Data Sources & Methodology

| Source | Usage | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| **U.S. Census Bureau ACS 2019-2023 (5-year estimates)** | Median household income by city | Most recent available; provides statistically reliable estimates for smaller geographies |
| **Field data (METHOD team)** | MPU and ROOF rates | Derived from actual site survey results across 66 deals |
| **Deal records** | All pipeline, financial, and performance metrics | 66 deals from Aug 2025 – Mar 2026 |

**Methodology notes:**
- Cancellation rates are calculated per city to reflect market-specific risk
- Install time uses a multiplicative model: `Base City Days × Monthly Efficiency Factor`, where the efficiency factor decreases over time as the team improves
- Socioeconomic brackets are derived from median household income relative to Bay Area cost of living
- All 10 cities are served by PG&E as the investor-owned utility (IOU)
- Deal values are generated from a distribution modeling real solar residential deal sizes in the Bay Area market
